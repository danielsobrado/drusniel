# APPENDIX G: THEMATIC THROUGHLINE â€” POWER AS ABSENCE

## The Pattern (Preserve This)

All major artifacts in this story feel like ABSENCE, not power:

| Artifact | Experience | Function |
|----------|------------|----------|
| **Erase (Null)** | Removes magical presence | User vanishes from detection |
| **Chassis (Cube)** | Creates void that pulls | Beacon broadcasts, draws pursuit |
| **Voice** | Attention without action | Awareness, not help |

## Why This Matters

**Traditional fantasy**: Power is addition. More magic, more strength, more capability.

**This story**: Power is REMOVAL. The artifacts take away. The debts are absence of freedom. The manipulation works by removing alternatives.

## Lean Into This

When writing prose:
- Artifacts should feel hungry, not full
- Power should feel like loss, not gain
- Drusniel's "growth" is actually subtraction (of innocence, of options, of agency)

**The cruel irony**: He thinks he's gaining power. He's actually being hollowed out.

This throughline should echo across both Drusniel and East Side arcs. When readers finish, they should feel that the Nexus artifacts don't GIVE anything. They TAKE.
