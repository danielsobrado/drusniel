## ACT I — CONVERGENCE (Chapters 0–4)

---

### CHAPTER 0 — *The Road from Zuraldi*  
**POV:** Dulint  
**Narrative Function:** The artifact transitions from inert curiosity to active danger.

**Subplots (5):**
1. The cube warms and rotates for the first time.
2. Dulint feels *absence*, not power.
3. A clear directional pull emerges (north-east).
4. Balin notices the change and challenges Dulint.
5. Distant figures appear behind them on the road.

**Reader State:**  
Something has awakened. No one knows what.

---

### CHAPTER 1 — *The Knot at Riverhold*  
**POV:** Aldric  
**Narrative Function:** Pattern recognition and the first vindication seed.

**Subplots (6):**
1. Aldric and Xandor discuss organized Grukmar movement.
2. Aldric's dismissal and bitterness resurface.
3. Dulint and Balin arrive seeking Xandor by name.
4. The cube is placed on the table and rotates sharply.
5. Direction aligns with Grukmar activity.
6. Aldric realizes this explains everything he warned about.

**Reader State:**  
The artifact explains enemy behavior, but not intent.

---

### CHAPTER 2 — *The Grass Where She Fell*  
**POV:** Maris Hale  
**Narrative Function:** Establish the physical cost of truth.

**Subplots (5):**
1. Nexus resonance strikes without warning.
2. Vision begins: drowning, pressure, darkness.
3. Full body shutdown — collapse, shallow breathing.
4. Bystanders believe she is dying.
5. Recovery is slow and incomplete.

**Reader State:**  
Knowledge is dangerous. Seeing truth may kill you.

---

### CHAPTER 3 — *The Circle Closes*  
**POV:** Dulint  
**Narrative Function:** The party forms under systemic pressure.

**Subplots (6):**
1. Maris enters the inn.
2. The cube reacts violently, briefly aligning toward her.
3. Xandor names the behavior: "It's reading."
4. Warning given — this will draw attention.
5. Aldric commits to protecting the artifact.
6. Someone outside the inn is listening.

**Reader State:**  
The artifact does not choose heroes — it uses proximity.

---

### CHAPTER 4 — *The Seer's Warning*  
**POV:** Dulint  
**Narrative Function:** Dulint's secret is introduced as future harm.

**Subplots (6):**
1. Dulint seeks a local seer alone.
2. The seer collapses on contact — same shutdown pattern.
3. Warning delivered: fail → Dulint dies, Stonehold burns.
4. The path named: north, then east, faster than comfort.
5. Dulint decides to tell no one.
6. Balin notices the lie.

**Reader State:**  
Withholding truth feels protective — but isn't.
